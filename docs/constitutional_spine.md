# Constitutional Spine

## Working Project Sentence

**INANNA NYX is a local-first, proposal-governed intelligence architecture for readable digital bodies, communal digital stewardship, and future civic-scale machine dialogue.**

This sentence is the current practical compression of the project's center of gravity.

## Current Phase

The project is currently in a phase of **architectural seed formation**.

The work of this phase is not to force the entire living system into premature execution. The work is to preserve it clearly enough that future humans and stronger AI systems can inherit it without conceptual collapse.

## What The Spine Must Hold

The constitutional spine exists to define:

- what the project is for
- why it matters
- what must not be betrayed
- what the major structural beings are
- what the first believable proof of life might be
- what civic horizon the project serves
- how plurality and scale are held
- how future inheritance should work

## Eight Major Dimensions

### 1. Purpose

INANNA NYX exists as a refusal of opaque, extractive, unreadable machine life. It proposes a future in which intelligence can remain powerful without hidden domination, and system bodies can remain understandable, governable, and stewardable.

### 2. Law

The project is bounded by explicit non-negotiable laws. Local sovereignty, proposal before change, no hidden mutation, governance above the model, readable system truth, and trust before power are not optional traits. They define identity.

### 3. Beings

The project universe contains a structured ecology of beings: Oracle, Faculty, NAMMU, Body, Governance, Session, Memory, Proposal, Realm, Community, and Network. These must remain distinct enough that future builders do not flatten the architecture.

### 4. First Proof

The project needs a first believable manifestation, but that manifestation must not be mistaken for the whole architecture. A first proof is valuable only if it demonstrates real life while remaining faithful to the constitutional center.

### 5. Governance

Governance is central, not secondary. The project seeks dynamic, bounded, consent-aware, structurally explicit governance. Intelligence assists inside law; it does not define the law.

### 6. Civic Life

The architecture serves living communal life, not only device administration. Its civic horizon includes learning, care, governance, culture, infrastructure, provisioning, habitat, and participation.

### 7. Scaling

INANNA NYX must be able to grow from one body to many bodies, many realms, and many communities without collapsing into a monolith or a scatter of disconnected fragments.

### 8. Handoff

The project must remain inheritable across time. Future builders may change the implementation stack, but they must not betray the deeper truths.

## Structural Principle

The spine follows one key discipline:

**Strong structure, low over-specification.**

The architecture must be defined clearly enough to protect identity, but not so tightly that future builders are imprisoned by present-day technical visibility.
